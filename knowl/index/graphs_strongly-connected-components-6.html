One graph algorithm that can help find clusters of highly interconnected vertices in a graph is called the strongly connected components algorithm (SCC). We formally define a strongly connected component, \(C\text{,}\) of a graph \(G\text{,}\) as the largest subset of vertices \(C \subset V\) such that for every pair of vertices \(v, w \in C\) we have a path from \(v\) to \(w\) and a path from \(w\) to \(v\text{.}\) FigureÂ 9.18.2 shows a simple graph with three strongly connected components. The strongly connected components are identified by the different shaded areas.
