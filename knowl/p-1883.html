When we split the list enough times, we end up with a list that has just one item. Either that is the item we are looking for or it is not. Either way, we are done. The number of comparisons necessary to get to this point is i where \(\frac {n}{2^i} =1\text{.}\) Solving for i gives us \(i=\log n\text{.}\) The maximum number of comparisons is logarithmic with respect to the number of items in the list. Therefore, the binary search is \(O(\log n)\text{.}\)
