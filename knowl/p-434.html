In order to implement a circuit such as Figure 1.13.5, we will first build a representation for logic gates. Logic gates are easily organized into a class inheritance hierarchy as shown in Figure 1.13.6. At the top of the hierarchy, the LogicGate class represents the most general characteristics of logic gates: namely, a label for the gate and an output line. The next level of subclasses breaks the logic gates into two families, those that have one input line and those that have two. Below that, the specific logic functions of each appear.
