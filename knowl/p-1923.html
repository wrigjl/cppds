Diagram illustrating the process of hashing a string using ordinal values. Three characters 'c', 'a', and 't' are shown at the top with arrows pointing down to their respective ordinal values: '99', '97', and '116'. These values are summed to equal '312'. An arrow then points from this sum to an operation '312 % 11', which results in '4'. This demonstrates a simple hash function where the sum of the character ordinals is divided by 11 to get a remainder, which serves as the hash value.
