To find the blue area, we can count the number of blue squares \(1+2+3+4+5+6+7+8\text{,}\) which we just learned can be written as \(\sum_{i=1}^{8} i.\) However, we also know how to find the area of a rectangle, by multiplying height by width, and the blue squares represent half of the rectangle. So, the area with blue squares is also just \(\sum_{i=1}^{8} i = \frac {(8)(8+1)}{2}\text{.}\)
