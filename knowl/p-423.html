FigureÂ 1.13.1 shows the built-in C++ collections and their relationships to one another. We call a relationship structure such as this an inheritance hierarchy. For example, the string is a child of the sequential collection. In this case, we call the string the child and the sequence the parent (or subclass string and superclass sequence). This is often referred to as an IS-A Relationship (the string IS-A sequential collection). This implies that strings inherit important characteristics from sequences, namely the ordering of the underlying data and operations such as concatenation, repetition, and indexing.
